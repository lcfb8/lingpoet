Behind the Words

Not a dictionary. Not a genealogy of language. We collect coincidences: words that look alike without borrowing, shared roots, or historical handshakes.
This website is an amalgamation of effort, curiosity, admiration, and work.

The Living Archives

The linguistic data comes from kaikki.org (kaikki — [Finnish] all, everything, everyone), a digital archive and data mining group who have extracted millions of words, and the information about those words, from Wiktionary.

Wiktionary is a free, collaborative, multilingual online dictionary which aims to describe all words of all languages using definitions and descriptions in English. Wiktionary is written by volunteer editors and hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation.

The Method (with AI help)

  • Download kaikki's extract of raw Wiktionary data (20GB).
  • From the raw data, create words.db, a database where each entry includes a word, language, pronunciation, and definition(s).
  • From words.db, create coincidences.db, a database of all matches by spelling and definition between languages, which drives the Wander game and the Words Studio.
  • Continually refine the build_coincidence_db.py script to filter out the words we don't want and keep only the coincidental matches (a work in progress).
Dive into the code: lingpoet on GitHub.

Assumptions & Caveats

We aim to filter for words with divergent meanings to exclude instances where the match was due to a shared etymological root or a loan word — an imperfect process that we continue to refine. We recognize that English is the dominant language of Wiktionary, and there are many words and languages out there that we are missing, particularly those written in scripts other than the Latin alphabet.
